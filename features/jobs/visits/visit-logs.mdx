## What Are Visit Logs?

Visit logs are detailed records of everything that happened during a visit - time spent, distance traveled, materials used, photos taken, and customer sign-off. They serve as:

- **Billing documentation** (how many hours to invoice)
- **Audit trail** (what work was performed and when)
- **Legal protection** (proof of completion, before/after photos)
- **Performance tracking** (tech efficiency, job profitability)

## Time Tracking

FieldCamp automatically tracks time at multiple stages of a visit:

### Time Categories

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Travel Time" icon="car" defaultOpen>
    **Definition:** Time from when tech leaves previous location until arriving at job site.
    
    **Tracking method:**
    - **Automatic (recommended):** Starts when status changes to "In Transit", stops when "Arrived"
    - **Manual:** Tech taps "Start Travel" and "End Travel"
    - **GPS-based:** Uses geofencing to auto-detect departure and arrival
    
    **Billable?** Depends on your pricing model:
    - **Flat-rate pricing:** Travel usually not billed separately
    - **Hourly pricing:** Travel may be billable (at same rate or reduced rate)
    - **Service call fee:** Travel included in fixed service call charge
    
    **Example:**
    ```
    Travel started: 9:47 AM (left previous job)
    Travel ended: 10:12 AM (arrived at job site)
    Total travel time: 25 minutes
    ```
    
    <Tip>
    **Pro tip:** Configure travel billing per job type:
    - **Emergency calls:** Travel time billable at 1.5x rate
    - **Maintenance contracts:** Travel time not billable (included in contract)
    - **Standard service:** Travel time billable at regular rate
    
    Settings → Job Types → [Select Type] → Travel Time Billing
    </Tip>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Work Time" icon="wrench">
    **Definition:** Time spent actively working on the job (excluding breaks).
    
    **Tracking method:**
    - **Automatic (recommended):** Starts when status changes to "In Progress", pauses during breaks, stops when "Completed"
    - **Manual:** Tech taps "Start Work" and "End Work"
    - **Multiple sessions:** If work is paused multiple times, all work sessions are summed
    
    **Billable?** Yes, always (this is primary billable time)
    
    **Example:**
    ```
    Session 1: 10:15 AM - 12:30 PM (2h 15m)
    Session 2: 1:00 PM - 2:45 PM (1h 45m)
    Total work time: 4 hours
    ```
    
    **Rounding rules:**
    - Default: Round to nearest 15 minutes (4h 7m → 4h 15m)
    - Optional: Round up to next 15 min (4h 1m → 4h 15m)
    - Optional: No rounding (exact time)
    
    Configure: **Settings → Time Tracking → Work Time Rounding**
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Break/Pause Time" icon="pause">
    **Definition:** Time when work is paused (lunch, waiting for parts, waiting for approval).
    
    **Tracking method:**
    - **Automatic:** Starts when status changes to "Paused", stops when "In Progress" resumed
    - **Categorized:** Tech selects pause reason from dropdown
    
    **Pause reasons:**
    - **Lunch break** (non-billable)
    - **Waiting for parts** (billable or non-billable, configurable)
    - **Waiting for customer approval** (usually billable)
    - **Weather delay** (non-billable)
    - **Equipment issue** (non-billable)
    - **Other** (custom)
    
    **Billable?** Depends on reason (configurable per reason)
    
    **Example:**
    ```
    Pause 1: Lunch break (12:30 PM - 1:00 PM) - 30 min - Non-billable
    Pause 2: Waiting for parts (1:45 PM - 2:15 PM) - 30 min - Billable
    Total pause time: 1 hour (30 min billable, 30 min non-billable)
    ```
    
    <Note>
    **Best practice:** Mark "Waiting for customer approval" as billable - the tech is on-site and ready to work, but waiting on the customer to make a decision.
    </Note>
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Total Time" icon="clock">
    **Definition:** Total time from travel start to work completion.
    
    **Calculation:**
    ```
    Total time = Travel time + Work time + Break time
    ```
    
    **Example:**
    ```
    Travel: 25 minutes
    Work: 4 hours
    Breaks: 1 hour (30 min billable, 30 min non-billable)
    Total time: 5 hours 25 minutes
    ```
    
    **Used for:**
    - Scheduling (did job take longer than estimated?)
    - Efficiency tracking (tech productivity)
    - Reporting (total time per job type, per tech, per day)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Billable Time" icon="dollar-sign">
    **Definition:** Time that will be invoiced to the customer.
    
    **Calculation:**
    ```
    Billable time = Work time + Billable pause time + Billable travel time (if configured)
    ```
    
    **Example (travel not billable):**
    ```
    Travel: 25 minutes (non-billable)
    Work: 4 hours (billable)
    Lunch break: 30 min (non-billable)
    Waiting for approval: 30 min (billable)
    Total billable time: 4.5 hours
    ```
    
    **Example (travel billable):**
    ```
    Travel: 25 minutes (billable)
    Work: 4 hours (billable)
    Lunch break: 30 min (non-billable)
    Waiting for approval: 30 min (billable)
    Total billable time: 4.92 hours (4h 55m)
    ```
    
    **Invoice line item:**
    ```
    Labor (4.5 hours @ $95/hour) = $427.50
    ```
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Auto-Start/Stop Timers (Mobile App)

FieldCamp mobile app automatically starts and stops timers based on visit status:

| Status Change | Timer Action |
|---------------|--------------|
| **Scheduled → In Transit** | Travel timer starts |
| **In Transit → Arrived** | Travel timer stops |
| **Arrived → In Progress** | Work timer starts |
| **In Progress → Paused** | Work timer pauses (pause timer starts) |
| **Paused → In Progress** | Pause timer stops, work timer resumes |
| **In Progress → Completed** | Work timer stops, all times finalized |

<Tip>
**Pro tip:** Techs don't need to manually track time! Just update status as they progress through the visit, and timers run automatically.

**Exception:** If a tech forgets to update status, they can manually edit time logs afterward (within 24-hour edit window).
</Tip>

### Manual Time Adjustments

Techs can manually adjust time if the auto-timer was incorrect:

**From mobile app:**
1. Tap on completed visit
2. Tap "Edit Time Log"
3. Adjust start/end times or add/remove pause periods
4. Add note explaining adjustment (e.g., "Forgot to mark as arrived, actually arrived at 10:05 AM")
5. Save changes

**Permissions:**
- **Tech:** Can edit own time logs within 24 hours of completion
- **Manager/Admin:** Can edit any time log at any time

**Audit trail:** All manual adjustments are logged with timestamp and user who made the change.

## Mileage Tracking

FieldCamp automatically tracks mileage using GPS:

### How GPS Mileage Tracking Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Tech Starts Travel">
    When tech changes status to "In Transit", GPS starts recording:
    - **Start location:** Current GPS coordinates (lat/lon)
    - **Start time:** Timestamp
    - **Start address:** Reverse-geocoded address (e.g., "123 Oak St")
  </Step>

  <Step title="GPS Tracks Route">
    As tech drives to job site:
    - GPS logs position every 30 seconds (configurable)
    - Route is recorded (breadcrumb trail)
    - Distance calculated using Haversine formula (accurate for curved Earth)
    
    **Accuracy:** ±0.1 miles (typical), ±0.3 miles (rural/poor GPS signal)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tech Arrives at Job Site">
    When tech changes status to "Arrived":
    - **End location:** GPS coordinates at job site
    - **End time:** Timestamp
    - **End address:** Job site address
    - **Total mileage:** Calculated distance from start to end
  </Step>

  <Step title="Return Trip (Optional)">
    Some companies track return mileage (job site → next job or home):
    - **Method 1:** Tech manually logs return mileage
    - **Method 2:** GPS auto-tracks when tech marks visit complete and starts traveling
    
    Configure: **Settings → Mileage Tracking → Track Return Mileage**
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Mileage Log Example

```
Visit: HVAC Repair - 456 Maple Ave

Outbound Trip:
Start: 9:47 AM - 123 Oak St (previous job)
End: 10:12 AM - 456 Maple Ave (job site)
Distance: 8.3 miles
Route: [GPS breadcrumb trail shown on map]

Return Trip: (not tracked - using default setting)

Total mileage: 8.3 miles
Reimbursement (@ $0.67/mile): $5.56
```

### Mileage Billing vs Reimbursement

**Reimbursement (most common):**
- Company reimburses tech for vehicle wear/tear and fuel
- Rate: $0.65-$0.70/mile (IRS standard mileage rate)
- Customer is NOT billed for mileage
- Used by: Most service companies

**Billable Mileage:**
- Company charges customer for travel distance
- Rate: $1.00-$3.00/mile (varies by industry)
- Separate invoice line item
- Used by: Some plumbing/HVAC companies, especially for rural areas

Configure: **Settings → Mileage Tracking → Billing Method**

### Manual Mileage Entry

If GPS is unavailable or inaccurate, techs can manually enter mileage:

**From mobile app:**
1. Complete visit
2. Tap "Edit Mileage"
3. Enter:
   - Start odometer reading (e.g., 45,320)
   - End odometer reading (e.g., 45,328)
   - Total: 8 miles (auto-calculated)
4. Add note: "GPS unavailable due to poor signal"
5. Save

**Permissions:** Techs can manually enter mileage, but all manual entries are flagged for manager review (to prevent fraud).

## Materials Used

Track products and parts used during a visit:

### Adding Materials from Mobile App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Visit in Mobile App">
    While visit status is "In Progress", tap **"Materials"** tab
  </Step>

  <Step title="Scan or Search for Product">
    **Option 1: Scan barcode** (if product has barcode in inventory)
    - Tap camera icon
    - Scan product barcode
    - Product auto-added
    
    **Option 2: Search by name**
    - Tap search bar
    - Type product name (e.g., "air filter")
    - Select from results
    
    **Option 3: Browse truck inventory**
    - View list of products on tech's truck
    - Tap product to add
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter Quantity">
    - Default: 1 unit
    - Increase/decrease quantity
    - Unit type auto-fills (each, box, gallon, etc.)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Price Auto-Fills from Catalog">
    - Cost price (what company paid)
    - Sell price (what customer pays)
    - Margin automatically calculated
    
    **Editable?** Yes, if tech has permission to adjust pricing on-site
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add to Visit Log">
    Tap **"Add"** → Material is logged with:
    - Product name and SKU
    - Quantity used
    - Price (cost and sell)
    - Timestamp (when added)
    - Tech who added it
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Materials Not in Catalog (Custom Items)

If tech uses a product not in your inventory:

1. Tap **"+ Add Custom Item"**
2. Enter:
   - Description (e.g., "3/4 inch copper elbow")
   - Quantity
   - Unit (each, box, linear foot, etc.)
   - Cost (optional)
   - Sell price
3. Save → Custom item added to visit log

<Note>
**Inventory sync:** When a product is added to a visit log, FieldCamp automatically:
- Deducts quantity from tech's truck inventory
- Adds quantity to job (for invoicing)
- Flags low-stock items (if tech only has 1 left)
</Note>

### Ordering Parts On-Site

If tech doesn't have a part on truck:

1. Tap **"Order Part"** in Materials tab
2. Search for part in catalog
3. Select quantity
4. Choose vendor (default vendor auto-selected)
5. Tap **"Order"** → Purchase order created
6. Office receives notification to order part
7. Visit status can be set to "Paused" (waiting for parts)

## Photos

Photos are critical for documentation, billing disputes, and quality control:

### Photo Types

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Before Photos" icon="camera">
    Condition of site/equipment before work begins
  </Card>
  <Card title="During Work Photos" icon="camera">
    Work in progress (e.g., inside equipment, wiring, damage found)
  </Card>
  <Card title="After Photos" icon="camera">
    Completed work, clean job site, customer satisfaction
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Taking Photos from Mobile App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Visit in Mobile App">
    Tap **"Photos"** tab (available at any visit status)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tap Camera Icon">
    Opens device camera (iOS/Android native camera)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Take Photo">
    Snap photo of:
    - Equipment (before/after)
    - Model/serial numbers (for warranty tracking)
    - Damage (before repair)
    - Completed work
    - Messy/clean site (before/after cleanup)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Caption (Optional)">
    - **Auto-caption (AI):** "HVAC unit before cleaning" (detected by image recognition)
    - **Manual caption:** "Thermostat wiring - customer installed wrong"
    - **No caption:** Just timestamp and GPS location
  </Step>

  <Step title="Categorize Photo">
    Select category:
    - **Before** (default if taken when status is "Arrived")
    - **During** (default if taken when status is "In Progress")
    - **After** (default if taken when status is "Completed")
    - **Equipment tag** (model/serial number photo)
    - **Damage**
    - **Other**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Photo Auto-Uploads">
    - **Online:** Uploads immediately (WiFi/cellular)
    - **Offline:** Queued for upload when connectivity restored
    
    Photo metadata automatically captured:
    - Timestamp (when photo was taken)
    - GPS coordinates (job site location)
    - Tech who took photo
    - Device used (iPhone 12, Samsung Galaxy, etc.)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Required Photos (Enforced)

You can require techs to take photos before completing a visit:

Navigate to **Settings → Job Types → [Select Type] → Required Photos**

**Example: HVAC Maintenance**
- **Required before photos:** 2 (equipment exterior, thermostat)
- **Required after photos:** 1 (cleaned unit)
- **Required equipment tag photos:** 1 (model/serial number)

**What happens:** If tech tries to mark visit complete without required photos, app shows error: "⚠️ 2 before photos required. You have taken 0."

<Tip>
**Best practice:** Require photos for:
- High-value jobs (>$500)
- Jobs with customer complaints history
- Warranty work (proof of completion)
- New tech training (manager can review)
</Tip>

### Photo Organization

Photos are automatically organized by:
- **Visit** (all photos from this visit)
- **Job** (all photos from all visits for this job)
- **Date** (all photos taken on this date)
- **Tech** (all photos taken by this tech)
- **Category** (all "before" photos, all "damage" photos, etc.)

**Viewing options:**
- **Gallery view:** Thumbnails (tap to enlarge)
- **Slideshow:** Swipe through photos
- **Before/After comparison:** Side-by-side view
- **Download all:** Zip file of all photos for this visit

### Photo Sharing

**With customer:**
- Include in invoice/receipt (before/after photos)
- Share via client portal (customer can download)
- Email directly to customer

**With team:**
- Share with manager for review
- Attach to internal note
- Use in training materials

**Privacy:** Customer-facing photos can be filtered (hide "equipment tag" or "during" photos that are for internal use only).

## Forms & Checklists

Techs complete forms/checklists during visits to document work performed:

### Types of Forms

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Service Checklists" icon="list-check">
    **Purpose:** Step-by-step checklist for standard services
    
    **Example: HVAC Maintenance Checklist**
    - ✅ Inspect air filter (clean or replace)
    - ✅ Check refrigerant levels
    - ✅ Lubricate moving parts
    - ✅ Test thermostat calibration
    - ✅ Inspect electrical connections
    - ✅ Measure airflow (CFM)
    - ✅ Customer walkthrough (explain findings)
    
    **Completion:** Tech checks off each item as they go
    
    **Result:** Form shows what was done (proof of service for customer)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Safety Checklists" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    **Purpose:** Ensure tech and customer safety
    
    **Example: Electrical Safety Checklist**
    - ✅ Power shut off before work
    - ✅ Circuit breaker tested
    - ✅ Grounding verified
    - ✅ Wiring inspected for damage
    - ✅ Voltage tested (multimeter)
    - ✅ GFI/AFCI working
    - ✅ Customer warned of hazards
    
    **Required:** Often required by insurance or regulatory compliance
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Inspection Forms" icon="clipboard-check">
    **Purpose:** Document condition of equipment/property
    
    **Example: Pool Inspection Form**
    - Water chemistry (pH, chlorine, alkalinity)
    - Equipment condition (pump, filter, heater)
    - Pool surface (cracks, stains, damage)
    - Deck/coping (safety hazards)
    - Recommendations (repairs needed)
    
    **Photos:** Often paired with photos for each inspection point
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Customer Surveys" icon="star">
    **Purpose:** Collect customer feedback
    
    **Example: Post-Service Survey**
    - How satisfied are you with the service? (1-5 stars)
    - Was the tech professional and courteous? (Yes/No)
    - Was the work area left clean? (Yes/No)
    - Would you recommend us? (Yes/No)
    - Comments (text field)
    
    **Collected by:** Tech asks customer to fill out on mobile device (or tech fills out based on customer responses)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Completing Forms on Mobile App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Visit in Mobile App">
    Tap **"Forms"** tab → See list of assigned forms
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Form">
    Tap form name (e.g., "HVAC Maintenance Checklist")
    
    Status indicator:
    - 🔴 **Not started** (0% complete)
    - 🟡 **In progress** (partial completion)
    - 🟢 **Completed** (100%)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Fill Out Fields">
    Form field types:
    - **Checkbox:** Check if task done (✅ or ⬜)
    - **Text input:** Enter notes (e.g., "Filter was very dirty, replaced")
    - **Number input:** Enter measurements (e.g., "240V")
    - **Dropdown:** Select from options (e.g., "Pass", "Fail", "N/A")
    - **Photo:** Attach photo to specific field (e.g., photo of air filter)
    - **Signature:** Customer or tech signature
    - **Date/time:** Auto-fills current date/time (editable)
    - **GPS location:** Auto-captures job site coordinates
  </Step>

  <Step title="Required Fields">
    Fields marked with ***** are required (can't complete form without them)
    
    **Example:**
    - "Air filter condition" (required - must select: Clean, Dirty, Replaced)
    - "Refrigerant level" (optional)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save Progress">
    - **Save draft:** Tap "Save" → form is partially complete (can finish later)
    - **Submit form:** Tap "Submit" → form is finalized (can't edit unless manager allows)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review & Export">
    Once submitted, form can be:
    - Viewed as PDF
    - Emailed to customer
    - Attached to invoice
    - Exported to CSV (for analytics)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
**Offline mode:** Forms can be filled out offline (no internet connection). Data is saved locally and auto-syncs when connectivity is restored.
</Note>

## Customer Signatures

Digital signatures confirm customer approval and job completion:

### When to Collect Signatures

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Required Signatures" icon="file-signature">
    **Work completion approval:**
    - Customer confirms work is done satisfactorily
    - Required before tech can mark visit complete
    
    **Additional work authorization:**
    - Customer approves extra charges beyond original quote
    - Required before tech can add unplanned items
    
    **Delivery/installation:**
    - Customer confirms equipment was delivered/installed
    - Required for warranty activation
  </Card>

  <Card title="Optional Signatures" icon="signature">
    **Customer satisfaction:**
    - Customer signs to indicate satisfaction (not legally required)
    
    **Safety acknowledgment:**
    - Customer acknowledges safety warnings
    
    **Payment receipt:**
    - Customer signs after payment (credit card, check)
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Capturing Signatures on Mobile App

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Signature Capture">
    **Method 1:** From visit detail → Tap "Get Signature"
    
    **Method 2:** From form → Signature field → Tap "Capture Signature"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tech Hands Device to Customer">
    Tech explains what customer is signing:
    
    **Example:**
    "Please sign here to confirm that we replaced your HVAC filter and the unit is working properly."
  </Step>

  <Step title="Customer Signs">
    - Customer uses finger (touchscreen) or stylus to sign
    - Signature appears on screen in real-time
    - Customer can tap "Clear" to re-sign if needed
  </Step>

  <Step title="Customer Confirms">
    After signing, customer taps **"Confirm"**
    
    **Captured data:**
    - Signature image (PNG format)
    - Timestamp (when signed)
    - GPS location (job site)
    - IP address (for legal proof)
    - Device ID (phone/tablet used)
    - Signee name (typed or auto-filled from customer record)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Tech Adds Context (Optional)">
    Tech can add note explaining what was signed:
    
    **Example:**
    "Customer approved additional $125 for duct repair"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Signature Saved">
    Signature is:
    - Attached to visit log
    - Included in invoice/receipt
    - Stored for legal/audit purposes
    - Emailed to customer (optional)
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Signature Types

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Work Completion Signature">
    **What it means:** Customer confirms work is complete and satisfactory
    
    **Legal value:** Proof that customer accepted the work (protects against later disputes)
    
    **Text above signature:**
    ```
    I confirm that [Tech Name] from [Company Name] has completed the following work:
    [Job description]
    
    I am satisfied with the quality of work.
    
    Signature: _______________  Date: __________
    Name (print): _____________
    ```
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Additional Work Authorization Signature">
    **What it means:** Customer approves additional charges beyond original quote
    
    **Legal value:** Proof that customer authorized extra work (prevents billing disputes)
    
    **Text above signature:**
    ```
    I authorize [Company Name] to perform the following additional work:
    - [Description of additional work]
    - Additional cost: $XXX
    
    I understand this will be added to my invoice.
    
    Signature: _______________  Date: __________
    Name (print): _____________
    ```
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Technician Signature">
    **What it means:** Tech confirms they performed the work
    
    **Legal value:** Accountability (tech can't deny they were on-site)
    
    **Use case:** Some forms require both customer AND tech signature
    
    **Example:** Warranty work (both parties confirm work was done according to warranty terms)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Warning>
**Legal disclaimer:** Digital signatures are legally binding in most jurisdictions (U.S. ESIGN Act, EU eIDAS). However, for high-value contracts (>$10,000), consult your lawyer about using additional verification (e.g., email confirmation, DocuSign).
</Warning>

## Editing Visit Logs

Visit logs can be edited after completion (with restrictions):

### Who Can Edit?

| Role | Edit Window | What Can Be Edited |
|------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Technician** | 24 hours after completion | Time, mileage, materials, photos, notes |
| **Manager** | Anytime | Everything (time, mileage, materials, photos, forms, signatures) |
| **Admin** | Anytime | Everything + can delete visit logs |
| **Office Staff** | Read-only | Can view but not edit |

### Edit Audit Trail

All edits are logged:

```
Visit Log Edit History:

2025-02-26 3:45 PM - Sarah (Tech)
  Changed: Work time from 3h 45m to 4h 0m
  Reason: "Forgot to stop timer, actually finished at 2:30 PM"

2025-02-27 9:15 AM - Mike (Manager)
  Changed: Added material "Air Filter - 20x25" (qty 1)
  Reason: "Tech forgot to log filter replacement"
```

**Why audit trail matters:**
- **Prevent fraud:** Manager can see if tech is inflating hours
- **Compliance:** Required for government contracts or audits
- **Dispute resolution:** Proof of what was originally logged vs edited

### Locking Visit Logs

To prevent further edits (e.g., after invoice is sent):

Navigate to visit detail page → Click **"⋯"** → **"Lock Visit Log"**

**What happens:**
- Visit log becomes read-only (even for managers)
- Only admins can unlock (with reason)
- Locked status shown on visit card

**Use case:** Lock visit logs after:
- Invoice is sent to customer
- Customer pays invoice
- 30 days have passed (to close out old logs)

Export visit logs for reporting, invoicing, or auditing:

### Export Formats

    - Time breakdown (travel, work, breaks)

### How to Export

**Single visit:**
1. Open visit detail page
2. Click **"⋯"** → **"Export"**
3. Choose format (PDF, CSV, JSON)
4. Download or email

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Tech forgot to stop timer - hours are inflated">
    **Solution:**
    
    1. Tech (or manager) opens visit detail
    2. Tap "Edit Time Log"
    3. Adjust end time to actual completion time
    4. Add note: "Forgot to stop timer, actually finished at 2:30 PM"
    5. Save → Billable hours recalculated
    
    **Prevention:** Enable auto-stop timer when visit marked complete (Settings → Time Tracking → Auto-Stop Timer)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="GPS mileage is way off (showing 50 miles for a 10-mile trip)">
    **Possible causes:**
    
    ✅ **Poor GPS signal:** Phone lost GPS lock and triangulated off cell towers (less accurate)
    
    ✅ **GPS drift:** Phone was stationary but GPS coordinates "drifted" (creates false movement)
    
    ✅ **Multiple trips:** Tech stopped at parts store mid-trip (added extra mileage)
    
    **Solution:**
    - Tech manually edits mileage to correct value
    - Manager reviews GPS route map to verify
    - If GPS is consistently inaccurate, switch to manual mileage entry for that tech
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Customer refuses to sign - what should tech do?">
    **Best practices:**
    
    ✅ **Explain what they're signing:** "This just confirms we finished the work today, not that you're paying right now"
    
    ✅ **Offer alternative:** "Can I have you initial here instead of a full signature?"
    
    ✅ **Document refusal:** If customer refuses, tech adds note: "Customer refused to sign, stated work is complete but does not want to sign"
    
    ✅ **Photo evidence:** Take photo of completed work as proof (customer can't dispute later)
    
    **When to escalate:** If customer refuses to sign AND disputes that work was done, manager should call customer immediately.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Photos aren't uploading from mobile app">
    **Troubleshooting:**
    
    ✅ **Check connectivity:** Photos only upload when online (WiFi or cellular)
    - Solution: Photos are queued locally and will auto-upload when connectivity restored
    
    ✅ **Check storage space:** Phone may be out of storage
    - Solution: Free up space or reduce photo quality (Settings → Mobile App → Photo Quality → Medium)
    
    ✅ **Check app permissions:** App may not have camera/storage permission
    - Solution: iOS Settings → FieldCamp → Enable Camera + Photos
    
    ✅ **Force sync:** Tap "Sync Now" in mobile app settings to retry upload
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
