## What Are Recurring Visits?

Recurring visits are visits that repeat on a regular schedule - weekly, monthly, quarterly, or custom intervals. They're ideal for maintenance contracts, subscription services, and any job that requires regular, ongoing service.

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="One-Time Visit" icon="calendar-check">
    Single scheduled appointment - created once, occurs once
  </Card>
  <Card title="Recurring Visit" icon="repeat">
    Series of visits - created once, occurs multiple times automatically
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Common Use Cases

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Pool Maintenance" icon="water-ladder" defaultOpen>
    **Frequency:** Weekly or bi-weekly
    
    **Service:** Pool cleaning, chemical balancing, equipment check
    
    **Example schedule:**
    - Every Monday at 10:00 AM
    - Duration: 1 hour
    - Assigned tech: Sarah
    - Continues until customer cancels contract
    
    **Billing:** Monthly subscription ($200/month for 4 weekly visits)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="HVAC Maintenance Contracts" icon="temperature-full">
    **Frequency:** Quarterly (every 3 months)
    
    **Service:** System inspection, filter replacement, tune-up
    
    **Example schedule:**
    - 1st Tuesday of every 3rd month at 2:00 PM
    - Duration: 2 hours
    - Assigned tech: Mike
    - Continues for 12 months (4 visits total)
    
    **Billing:** Annual contract ($400/year prepaid, or $110 per visit)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Lawn Care / Landscaping" icon="seedling">
    **Frequency:** Weekly (seasonal - April to October)
    
    **Service:** Mowing, edging, blowing
    
    **Example schedule:**
    - Every Wednesday at 8:00 AM
    - Duration: 45 minutes
    - Assigned tech: Tom
    - Ends October 31st (28 visits total)
    
    **Billing:** Weekly or monthly ($40 per visit, or $160/month)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Pest Control" icon="bugs">
    **Frequency:** Monthly or quarterly
    
    **Service:** Interior/exterior spraying, inspection
    
    **Example schedule:**
    - 3rd Friday of each month at 1:00 PM
    - Duration: 1 hour
    - Assigned tech: Rotating (whoever is available)
    - Continues for 12 months (annual contract)
    
    **Billing:** Monthly subscription ($75/month)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Equipment Inspections" icon="clipboard-check">
    **Frequency:** Annually or semi-annually
    
    **Service:** Fire extinguisher inspection, elevator inspection, safety audit
    
    **Example schedule:**
    - Every 6 months on January 15th and July 15th at 9:00 AM
    - Duration: 3 hours
    - Assigned tech: Certified inspector
    - Continues indefinitely (as long as customer has contract)
    
    **Billing:** Per inspection ($250 each)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Creating Recurring Visits

### Method 1: From Calendar (Visual Scheduling)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Calendar">
    Navigate to **Calendar** → Choose week or month view
  </Step>

  <Step title="Click Time Slot">
    Click on the date and time when you want the first visit to occur
    
    **Example:** Monday, March 3rd at 10:00 AM
  </Step>

  <Step title="Enable Recurring">
    In the "Create Visit" modal, check the box: **☑ Recurring Visit**
    
    The form expands to show recurrence options.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Job">
    Choose the job this visit series is for:
    
    - **Existing job:** Select from dropdown (e.g., "Pool Maintenance - 123 Oak St")
    - **New job:** Create a new job first, then schedule recurring visits
    
    <Tip>
    **Pro tip:** Create a "Master Job" for the entire contract (e.g., "Annual HVAC Maintenance Contract"), then schedule recurring visits under that job. This keeps all visits grouped together.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Recurrence Pattern">
    Choose how often visits repeat:
    
    <AccordionGroup>
      <Accordion title="Daily (Rare)">
        **Repeats:** Every X days
        
        **Example:** Every 1 day (daily), Every 2 days (every other day)
        
        **Use case:** Rare in field services, but useful for multi-day projects or daily site monitoring
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Weekly (Most Common)">
        **Repeats:** Every X weeks on specific day(s)
        
        **Example:** Every 1 week on Monday (every Monday), Every 2 weeks on Monday (bi-weekly)
        
        **Multiple days:** Can select multiple days (e.g., every Monday and Friday)
        
        **Use case:** Pool maintenance, lawn care, cleaning services
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Monthly (Common)">
        **Two options:**
        
        **Option 1: Day of month**
        - Every X months on the [1st, 15th, last day]
        - Example: Every 1 month on the 15th (15th of each month)
        
        **Option 2: Day of week**
        - Every X months on the [1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, last] [Monday, Tuesday, etc.]
        - Example: Every 1 month on the 2nd Tuesday (2nd Tuesday of each month)
        
        **Use case:** Pest control, equipment inspections
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Quarterly (Every 3 Months)">
        **Repeats:** Every 3 months on specific date
        
        **Example:** Every 3 months on the 1st (Jan 1, Apr 1, Jul 1, Oct 1)
        
        **Use case:** HVAC seasonal maintenance, quarterly inspections
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Yearly (Annual)">
        **Repeats:** Every year on specific date
        
        **Example:** Every year on March 15th
        
        **Use case:** Annual inspections, certifications, contract renewals
      </Accordion>

      <Accordion title="Custom (Advanced)">
        **Repeats:** Every X days/weeks/months with custom rules
        
        **Example:** Every 10 days, Every 5 weeks on Wednesday, Every 4 months on the last Friday
        
        **Use case:** Non-standard contracts, seasonal schedules
      </Accordion>
    </AccordionGroup>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Start Date">
    **First visit date:** Defaults to the date you clicked in calendar
    
    **Can be changed:** If you want to create the series now but start visits later
    
    **Example:** Today is March 1st, but first visit should be April 1st
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set End Condition">
    When should recurring visits stop?
    
    <CardGroup cols={3}>
      <Card title="Never" icon="infinity">
        Visits continue indefinitely until manually cancelled
        
        **Use case:** Ongoing maintenance contracts, subscriptions
      </Card>

      <Card title="After X Occurrences" icon="hashtag">
        Stop after a specific number of visits
        
        **Example:** After 12 occurrences (1 year of monthly visits)
        
        **Use case:** Annual contracts with fixed visit count
      </Card>

      <Card title="Until Date" icon="calendar-xmark">
        Stop on a specific end date
        
        **Example:** Until October 31, 2025 (end of lawn care season)
        
        **Use case:** Seasonal services, contract end dates
      </Card>
    </CardGroup>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Assign Technician">
    **Option 1: Same tech for all visits** (recommended for consistency)
    - Select one tech (e.g., Sarah)
    - All visits in the series assigned to Sarah
    
    **Option 2: Rotating techs**
    - Select "Rotate" and choose 2+ techs
    - Visits alternate between techs (Visit 1: Sarah, Visit 2: Mike, Visit 3: Sarah, etc.)
    
    **Option 3: Auto-assign (no tech selected)**
    - Visits created without tech assignment
    - Dispatcher assigns techs later (more flexible but requires manual work)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Set Duration & Time">
    **Start time:** Same for all visits (e.g., 10:00 AM)
    
    **Duration:** Same for all visits (e.g., 1 hour)
    
    <Note>
    **Time consistency:** Customers appreciate predictable schedules (same time each week). Only change start time if absolutely necessary.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add Visit Notes (Optional)">
    Internal notes for technicians:
    
    **Example:** "Customer prefers service in the morning. Dogs in backyard - use front gate."
    
    These notes are copied to all visits in the series.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Review & Create">
    FieldCamp shows a preview of the series:
    
    ```
    Recurring Visit Series:
    - Job: Pool Maintenance - 123 Oak St
    - Pattern: Every 1 week on Monday at 10:00 AM
    - Start: March 3, 2025
    - End: Never (ongoing)
    - Tech: Sarah
    - Estimated visits: 52 per year
    
    Next 5 visits:
    1. Monday, March 3 at 10:00 AM
    2. Monday, March 10 at 10:00 AM
    3. Monday, March 17 at 10:00 AM
    4. Monday, March 24 at 10:00 AM
    5. Monday, March 31 at 10:00 AM
    ```
    
    Click **"Create Recurring Visits"** to finalize.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Visits Generated">
    **What happens:**
    - All visits are created and added to calendar
    - Tech receives notification (one notification for the series, not per visit)
    - Customer receives confirmation (optional)
    - Visits appear on calendar with a "recurrence" icon (🔁)
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Managing Recurring Visit Series

After creating a recurring visit series, you can edit, pause, or cancel:

### Editing Recurring Visits

When you edit a visit that's part of a recurring series, you'll see three options:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Edit This Visit Only" icon="calendar-day">
    **What changes:** Only the specific visit you clicked on
    
    **Use case:**
    - Reschedule one visit due to holiday or customer conflict
    - Assign different tech for one visit (regular tech is on vacation)
    - Change time for one visit (customer has appointment)
    
    **Example:**
    - Regular schedule: Every Monday at 10:00 AM
    - Customer requests: Move March 17th visit to Tuesday March 18th at 2:00 PM
    - Action: Edit March 17th visit only → move to March 18th
    - Result: All other visits remain on Monday at 10:00 AM
    
    **Future visits:** Unaffected
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Edit This and All Future Visits" icon="calendar-arrow-up">
    **What changes:** The selected visit and all subsequent visits in the series
    
    **Use case:**
    - Customer requests permanent time change (10am → 2pm going forward)
    - Switch to different tech starting from a specific date
    - Change visit duration for remaining visits
    
    **Example:**
    - Regular schedule: Every Monday at 10:00 AM
    - Customer requests: Starting April 1st, change to 2:00 PM
    - Action: Edit April 1st visit → Select "This and all future visits" → Change time to 2:00 PM
    - Result:
      - Visits before April 1st: Still at 10:00 AM
      - Visits April 1st and later: Now at 2:00 PM
    
    **Past visits:** Unaffected (already completed or locked)
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Edit All Visits in Series" icon="calendar">
    **What changes:** Every visit in the series (past, present, future)
    
    **Use case:**
    - Change recurrence pattern (weekly → bi-weekly)
    - Reassign all visits to different tech
    - Change job (if job details were updated)
    
    **Example:**
    - Regular schedule: Every 1 week on Monday
    - Customer downgrades contract: Change to every 2 weeks
    - Action: Edit any visit → Select "All visits in series" → Change pattern to "Every 2 weeks"
    - Result: System deletes every other visit, keeps only bi-weekly visits
    
    <Warning>
    **Caution:** Editing all visits affects completed visits too. If visits have already been completed and logged, you may not want to change those. Use "This and all future visits" instead.
    </Warning>
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

### Pausing Recurring Visits

Temporarily stop recurring visits (e.g., customer on vacation, seasonal break):

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Recurring Series Settings">
    Calendar → Click any visit in the series → **"⋯"** → **"Manage Recurrence"**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select 'Pause Series'">
    Choose pause start and end dates:
    
    **Example:**
    - Pause from: June 1, 2025
    - Resume on: July 1, 2025
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm">
    **What happens:**
    - All visits between June 1 and July 1 are cancelled
    - Visits after July 1 remain scheduled
    - Customer is notified (optional)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Resume Series">
    On July 1st, recurring visits automatically resume
    
    **Or manually resume early:**
    - Open series settings → **"Resume Series"**
  </Step>
</Steps>

**Use cases:**
- Customer vacation (pause for 2 weeks)
- Seasonal break (lawn care paused Nov-March)
- Equipment offline (pool heater repair, pause pool service)

## Cancelling Recurring Visits

Stop the recurring series permanently:

### Full Cancellation (All Remaining Visits)

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Recurring Series">
    Calendar → Click any future visit → **"⋯"** → **"Cancel Series"**
  </Step>

  <Step title="Choose Cancellation Type">
    **Option 1: Cancel all future visits**
    - Keeps past completed visits
    - Cancels all upcoming visits
    
    **Option 2: Cancel entire series**
    - Deletes all visits (past and future)
    - Use only if series was created by mistake
  </Step>

  <Step title="Select Reason">
    **Common reasons:**
    - Customer cancelled contract
    - Service no longer needed
    - Customer moved
    - Switched to competitor
    - Seasonal end (lawn care season over)
    
    Reason is logged for reporting and customer retention analysis.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Notify Customer">
    **Optional:** Send cancellation confirmation to customer
    
    **Email/SMS includes:**
    - Cancellation date
    - Last completed visit
    - Remaining balance (if prepaid contract)
    - Next steps (refund, final invoice, etc.)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm Cancellation">
    Click **"Cancel Recurring Visits"**
    
    **What happens:**
    - All future visits are cancelled
    - Tech is notified (calendar clears)
    - Customer is notified (if enabled)
    - Cancelled visits remain in history (for reporting)
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
**Cancellation grace period:** Enable a 24-hour grace period before cancellations take effect (prevents accidental cancellations).

Settings → Visits → Recurring Visit Cancellation → Require 24-hour notice
</Tip>

## Auto-Scheduling vs Manual Scheduling

When creating recurring visits, you can choose how visits are generated:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Auto-Scheduling (Recommended)" icon="wand-magic-sparkles">
    **How it works:** System automatically generates visits based on recurrence pattern
    
    **Example:** Create series on March 1st → System creates all 52 weekly visits for the year
    
    **Pros:**
    - ✅ All visits visible on calendar immediately
    - ✅ Techs can see their full schedule ahead
    - ✅ Customers can see all upcoming visits
    - ✅ No manual work required
    
    **Cons:**
    - ❌ Large series (100+ visits) may clutter calendar
    - ❌ If pattern changes, must edit all future visits
    
    **Best for:** Fixed contracts with known end date
  </Card>

  <Card title="Rolling Auto-Scheduling" icon="arrows-rotate">
    **How it works:** System generates visits X weeks/months ahead (e.g., 4 weeks ahead)
    
    **Example:** On March 1st, system creates visits for March 1-31. On April 1st, creates April visits.
    
    **Pros:**
    - ✅ Calendar stays cleaner (only shows next month)
    - ✅ Easier to adjust pattern (fewer future visits to edit)
    - ✅ Adapts to schedule changes automatically
    
    **Cons:**
    - ❌ Techs can't see full schedule beyond X weeks
    - ❌ Requires periodic system check (creates visits on schedule)
    
    **Best for:** Ongoing contracts with no end date
    
    Configure: **Settings → Recurring Visits → Auto-Schedule Ahead → 4 weeks**
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Recurring Visit Templates

Save recurring patterns as templates for faster setup:

### Creating a Template

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Recurring Visit (Once)">
    Set up a recurring visit with your desired pattern (e.g., weekly pool maintenance)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Save as Template">
    After creating, click **"⋯"** → **"Save as Template"**
    
    Name the template: "Weekly Pool Maintenance"
  </Step>

  <Step title="Template Saves:">
    - Recurrence pattern (every 1 week on Monday)
    - Duration (1 hour)
    - Visit notes
    - Forms/checklists to assign
    - Job items (if applicable)
  </Step>

  <Step title="Use Template for New Customers">
    When setting up a new pool maintenance customer:
    1. Create job for customer
    2. Schedule visit → Select template: "Weekly Pool Maintenance"
    3. Confirm start date and tech
    4. Done! All pattern details auto-fill
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Recurring Visit Analytics

Track performance and revenue from recurring visits:

### Key Metrics

Navigate to **Reports → Recurring Visits**

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Completion Rate" icon="percent">
    **What it measures:** % of recurring visits completed on schedule
    
    **Formula:** (Completed visits / Scheduled visits) × 100
    
    **Example:** 48 out of 52 weekly visits completed = 92% completion rate
    
    **Why it matters:** Low completion rate indicates scheduling issues or customer cancellations
  </Card>

  <Card title="Revenue per Series" icon="dollar-sign">
    **What it measures:** Total revenue from each recurring visit series
    
    **Example:** Pool maintenance contract = $200/month × 12 months = $2,400/year
    
    **Why it matters:** Recurring revenue is more predictable and valuable than one-time jobs
  </Card>

  <Card title="Churn Rate" icon="arrow-right-from-bracket">
    **What it measures:** % of recurring series cancelled before completion
    
    **Formula:** (Cancelled series / Total series) × 100
    
    **Example:** 5 out of 50 pool contracts cancelled = 10% churn rate
    
    **Why it matters:** High churn indicates customer dissatisfaction or competitive pressure
  </Card>

  <Card title="Average Series Duration" icon="calendar-days">
    **What it measures:** How long customers keep recurring contracts
    
    **Example:** Average pool contract lasts 18 months
    
    **Why it matters:** Longer duration = higher customer lifetime value (CLV)
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

### Recurring Revenue Report

**Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR):**
```
January 2025 MRR:
- 25 pool contracts @ $200/month = $5,000
- 10 lawn contracts @ $160/month = $1,600
- 8 pest contracts @ $75/month = $600
Total MRR: $7,200

Annual Run Rate (ARR): $7,200 × 12 = $86,400
```

**Why MRR matters:**
- Predictable revenue (easier to forecast)
- Business valuation (SaaS-style multiples: 3-5× ARR)
- Cash flow stability (recurring payments vs one-time jobs)

## Best Practices

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Assign Same Tech Each Visit" icon="user-check">
    **Why:** Customers appreciate consistency (builds trust and relationship)
    
    **How:** Assign tech when creating series, only rotate if absolutely necessary
  </Card>

  <Card title="Use Same Day/Time" icon="clock">
    **Why:** Predictability for customer (they know "every Monday at 10am")
    
    **How:** Avoid changing time unless customer requests it
  </Card>

  <Card title="Set Realistic End Dates" icon="calendar-check">
    **Why:** Contracts should have clear end dates (auto-renew vs manual renewal)
    
    **How:** For annual contracts, set end date 1 year out (then renew if customer continues)
  </Card>

  <Card title="Monitor Completion Rates" icon="chart-line">
    **Why:** Low completion = scheduling issues or customer dissatisfaction
    
    **How:** Weekly report of all recurring visits (completed vs skipped)
  </Card>

  <Card title="Automate Billing" icon="credit-card">
    **Why:** Recurring visits = recurring invoices (reduce manual work)
    
    **How:** Set up auto-invoicing (monthly charge for all visits that month)
  </Card>

  <Card title="Proactive Communication" icon="comment">
    **Why:** Remind customers before each visit (reduces no-shows)
    
    **How:** Enable auto-reminders (24 hours before visit)
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Troubleshooting

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Customer wants to change from weekly to bi-weekly - how do I update?">
    **Solution:**
    
    1. Open any future visit in the series
    2. Click **"⋯"** → **"Manage Recurrence"**
    3. Select **"Edit This and All Future Visits"**
    4. Change pattern: Every 1 week → Every 2 weeks
    5. Save
    
    **Result:** System deletes every other future visit, keeps only bi-weekly visits
    
    **Customer notification:** Send email explaining schedule change and new visit dates
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Tech quit - how do I reassign all their recurring visits?">
    **Solution:**
    
    1. Navigate to **Calendar** → Filter by departing tech
    2. Select all future visits (bulk select)
    3. Click **"Bulk Actions"** → **"Reassign"**
    4. Choose new tech (or multiple techs if splitting workload)
    5. Confirm
    
    **Result:** All future visits reassigned, new tech receives notification
    
    **Customer notification:** "Your regular technician [Old Tech] is no longer with us. [New Tech] will be handling your service going forward."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Customer paid for 12 visits upfront, but cancelled after 6 - how do I handle?">
    **Billing approach:**
    
    1. Cancel remaining 6 visits (use "Cancel Series")
    2. Calculate refund:
       - Total paid: $1,200 (12 visits @ $100 each)
       - Completed: 6 visits = $600
       - Refund due: $600
    3. Issue refund via your payment processor
    4. Send customer confirmation of refund
    
    **Or offer credit instead of refund:**
    - "We'll credit your account $600 for future service if you decide to resume"
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Recurring visits aren't showing up on calendar">
    **Troubleshooting:**
    
    ✅ **Check filter:** Calendar may be filtered by a specific tech (clear filters)
    
    ✅ **Check date range:** Calendar may be showing only this week (expand to month view)
    
    ✅ **Check auto-scheduling setting:** If using rolling auto-schedule (4 weeks ahead), visits beyond that aren't created yet
    
    ✅ **Check series status:** Series may be paused or cancelled (check series settings)
    
    ✅ **Force refresh:** Click "Sync" button to reload calendar data
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
