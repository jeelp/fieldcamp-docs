# Creating Jobs in FieldCamp

Creating a job is the first step in every field service workflow. Whether you're scheduling a simple repair or planning a complex installation, FieldCamp makes it fast and flexible.

<Info>
  **New to jobs?** Read [Jobs Overview](/features/jobs/overview) first to understand the basics.
</Info>

---

## Quick Start: Create a Job in 30 Seconds

The fastest way to create a job:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + New Job">
    From the **Jobs** page or dashboard, click the **+ New Job** button.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Client">
    Choose a client from the dropdown (or create a new one).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Description">
    Enter what needs to be done (e.g., "Fix leaky faucet in kitchen").
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Create Job**. Done!
  </Step>
</Steps>

That's all you need for a basic job. Everything else (pricing, scheduling, team assignment) is optional and can be added later.

### Required Fields

Only **two fields** are required to create a job:

#### 1. Client

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Select Existing Client">
    Start typing the client's name or company. FieldCamp will auto-suggest matching clients.
    
    When you select a client, their address, phone number, and email auto-populate.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Create New Client">
    Don't see the client? Click **+ New Client** to create one inline.
    
    You'll need:
    - Client name (required)
    - Phone number (recommended)
    - Address (recommended)
    
    Everything else can be added later.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Add clients before creating jobs. It's faster and helps you keep client data clean.
</Tip>

<!-- Screenshot: client-selector-dropdown.png — Client selector with search and new client button -->

---

#### 2. Description

A clear description of what needs to be done. This shows up:
- In the mobile app (for techs)
- On invoices and estimates
- In job search results
- In client-facing documents

**Good examples:**
- ✅ "Replace water heater with 50-gallon electric model. Inspect and replace expansion tank if needed."
- ✅ "AC tune-up: clean coils, check refrigerant, replace filter, test thermostat"
- ✅ "Fix leaky faucet in master bathroom sink. Client reports slow drain as well — inspect."

**Bad examples:**
- ❌ "Water heater" (too vague)
- ❌ "Job 123" (not descriptive)
- ❌ "Same as last time" (unclear)

<Warning>
  The description is the first thing techs see on their mobile app. Make it clear and specific so they know what to expect.
</Warning>

### Optional Fields

These fields are optional but recommended for better organization and tracking:

#### Job Type

Choose between **One-Time** (default) or **Recurring**.

    - Weekly lawn care

    - **Frequency:** Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, Yearly, Custom

    - **End condition:** Never, After X occurrences, or Until a specific date

#### Why Add Items Now?

Adding items when you create a job helps you:
- ✅ Give accurate quotes to clients upfront
- ✅ Track inventory needs (know what parts to bring)
- ✅ Generate invoices automatically later (items become invoice line items)

<Tip>
  You can always add items later from the job detail page.

<Steps>

  <Step title="Set Quantity">

<Steps>

## Creating Recurring Jobs

For ongoing maintenance or subscription-based services, create a recurring job.

### Step 1: Set Job Type to Recurring

In the create job modal, select **Recurring** instead of One-Time.

---

### Step 2: Configure Recurrence Pattern

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Daily">
    **Example:** Daily pool service for a hotel
    
    - **Every X days:** 1 (daily), 2 (every other day), etc.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Weekly">
    **Example:** Weekly lawn care every Friday
    
    - **Every X weeks:** 1 (weekly), 2 (bi-weekly), 4 (monthly)
    - **On days:** Monday, Tuesday, etc. (select multiple)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Monthly">
    **Example:** Monthly HVAC maintenance on the 1st
    
    - **Day of month:** 1–31 (e.g., "15th of every month")
    - **OR Day of week:** "First Monday", "Last Friday", etc.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Quarterly">
    **Example:** Quarterly fire extinguisher inspection
    
    - **Start month:** January, April, July, October
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Yearly">
    **Example:** Annual HVAC tune-up every spring
    
    - **Month and day:** March 1, May 15, etc.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Custom">
    **Example:** Every 45 days, every 3 weeks, etc.
    
    - **Frequency:** Days, Weeks, Months
    - **Interval:** Number of periods between occurrences
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<!-- Screenshot: recurrence-pattern-selector.png — Recurrence settings with frequency options -->

---

### Step 3: Set End Condition

Choose when the recurring job should stop:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Never">
    Job repeats indefinitely until you manually cancel it.
    
    **Use for:** Ongoing maintenance contracts with no end date.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="After X occurrences">
    Job stops after a specific number of visits.
    
    **Example:** "After 12 visits" for a 1-year monthly contract.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Until a specific date">
    Job stops after a certain date.
    
    **Example:** "Until December 31, 2026" for a calendar-year contract.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<!-- Screenshot: end-condition-selector.png — End condition options with date picker -->

---

### Step 4: Auto-Create Visits (Optional)

Check **Auto-create visits** to have FieldCamp automatically schedule visits on the recurrence pattern.

**How it works:**
- FieldCamp creates visits in advance (configurable, default: 30 days)
- Visits appear on the calendar and in the mobile app
- You can reschedule or cancel individual visits without affecting the series

<Warning>
  If you **don't** check auto-create visits, the job is marked recurring but you'll need to manually schedule each visit.
</Warning>

<Info>
  Learn more: [Recurring Visits](/features/jobs/visits/recurring-visits)

## Bulk Job Creation

Need to create many jobs at once? Use bulk import.

### Step 1: Prepare a CSV File

Your CSV should have these columns:

| Column | Required? | Description |
|--------|-----------|-------------|
| `clientName` | ✅ Yes | Client name (must match existing client) |
| `description` | ✅ Yes | What needs to be done |
| `status` | No | Draft, Scheduled, etc. (default: Draft) |
| `priority` | No | Low, Medium, High, Urgent (default: Medium) |
| `tags` | No | Comma-separated tags |
| `assignedTo` | No | Tech email or name |

**Example CSV:**

```csv
clientName,description,status,priority,tags,assignedTo
ABC Plumbing,Monthly AC maintenance,Unscheduled,Medium,HVAC,john@example.com
XYZ Electric,Fix outlet in kitchen,Draft,Low,Electrical,jane@example.com
```

---

### Step 2: Import

1. Go to **Jobs** page
2. Click **⋮ More** → **Import Jobs**
3. Upload your CSV
4. Map columns (FieldCamp auto-detects)
5. Click **Import**

<!-- Screenshot: bulk-import-ui.png — CSV import interface with column mapping -->

<Warning>
  Bulk import requires **Owner** or **Admin** role.
</Warning>

## Troubleshooting

### I can't find a client in the dropdown

**Possible causes:**
1. Client doesn't exist yet → Create a new client
2. Client is archived → Go to [Clients](/features/clients/overview) and unarchive
3. Spelling mismatch → Try searching by phone number or company name

---

### AI Dispatcher isn't showing up

**Possible causes:**
1. No job address → AI needs GPS coordinates to calculate drive time
2. No available techs → All techs are busy or off during the visit time
3. Skills required but no techs have them → Remove skill requirement or assign manually

<Info>
  Learn more: [AI Dispatcher Troubleshooting](/features/jobs/ai-dispatcher#troubleshooting)
</Info>

---

### Items aren't showing in the picker

**Possible causes:**
1. Your catalog is empty → Go to [Products & Services](/features/product-service/overview) and add items
2. Items are archived → Unarchive them from the catalog
3. Search term doesn't match → Try broader search

---

### Job created but doesn't appear in the list

**Possible causes:**
1. Job status is Draft and your view filters Drafts → Change view or filter
2. You created the job for a different organization (multi-org users) → Check org selector
3. Your role can only see assigned jobs → Check that you're assigned to the job

## API: Create Jobs Programmatically

Create jobs via the FieldCamp API:

```bash
curl -X POST https://app.fieldcamp.ai/api/v1/jobs \
  -H "X-Api-Key: your_api_key" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "clientId": "507f1f77bcf86cd799439011",
    "description": "Fix leaky faucet in kitchen",
    "status": "Draft",
    "priority": "Medium",
    "tags": ["Plumbing", "Emergency"],
    "items": [
      {
        "name": "Kitchen Faucet - Chrome",
        "quantity": 1,
        "unitPrice": 150.00,
        "taxRate": 0.10
      },
      {
        "name": "Labor - Plumbing",
        "quantity": 1,
        "unitPrice": 120.00,
        "taxRate": 0.10
      }
    ]
  }'
```

```json
// Response
{
  "success": true,
  "data": {
    "id": "507f1f77bcf86cd799439012",
    "jobNumber": "JOB-1234",
```
