# Job Items & Pricing

Job items are the billable products and services you provide to clients. Understanding how to add, price, discount, and tax items is essential for accurate invoicing and profitability tracking.

<Info>
  **New to jobs?** Read [Jobs Overview](/features/jobs/overview) first to understand the basics.
</Info>

---

## What are Job Items?

**Job items** are the products and services you add to a job. Each item has:

- **Name** (what it is)
- **Quantity** (how many)
- **Unit Price** (price per unit)
- **Tax** (if applicable)
- **Total** (quantity × price + tax)

**Examples:**
- **Product:** "Water Heater - 50 Gallon Electric" × 1 @ $800 = $800
- **Service:** "Labor - Plumbing" × 2 hours @ $120/hour = $240
- **Material:** "PVC Pipe - 1 inch" × 10 feet @ $3/foot = $30

## Adding Items to Jobs

You can add items when creating a job or later from the job detail page.

### Adding Items from Your Catalog

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + Add Item">
    In the job's **Details** tab, click **+ Add Item**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Search Catalog">
    Start typing the product or service name. FieldCamp searches your catalog.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Item">
    Click the item you want. Price, tax rate, and description auto-fill.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Quantity">
    Enter how many units (default: 1).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Adjust Price (Optional)">
    Override the default price if needed (custom pricing for this job).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Add**. The item appears in the job's item list.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<!-- Screenshot: add-item-modal.png — Item picker with search and catalog results -->

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Add items to your catalog first from [Products & Services](/features/product-service/overview). It's faster to select from catalog than to create custom items every time.
</Tip>

---

### Creating Custom Items (Ad-Hoc)

Don't have the item in your catalog? Create a custom one:

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + Custom Item">
    In the item picker, click **+ Custom Item**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Details">
    - **Name:** "Emergency weekend service fee"
    - **Unit Price:** $200
    - **Quantity:** 1
    - **Tax:** Choose tax rate or "No tax"
    - **Description (Optional):** Internal notes or customer-facing description
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add">
    Click **Add**. The item is added to this job only (not saved to catalog).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  To add the item to your catalog for future use, go to [Products & Services](/features/product-service/overview) and create it there.
</Info>

<!-- Screenshot: custom-item-form.png — Custom item creation form with fields -->

### Adding Kits / Bundles

Kits are pre-configured bundles of multiple items.

**Example:** "HVAC Tune-Up Kit" includes:
- Filter replacement
- Coil cleaning
- Refrigerant check
- Thermostat calibration
- Labor (2 hours)

**How to add a kit:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + Add Kit">
    In the item picker, click **+ Add Kit**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Kit">
    Choose from your saved kits.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Review Items">
    All items in the kit are added to the job at once.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Adjust Quantities (Optional)">
    Edit individual items if needed.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Learn how to create kits: [Products & Services - Kits](/features/product-service/kits)
</Info>

## Item Types

### Products

Physical items you sell or install.

**Examples:**
- Water heaters
- HVAC units
- Faucets
- Light fixtures
- Parts (filters, belts, hoses)

**Characteristics:**
- Have inventory tracking (optional)
- Have a cost (what you paid) and price (what you charge)
- May have SKU, vendor, category

---

### Services

Labor, hourly work, or flat-rate services.

**Examples:**
- "Labor - Plumbing" (hourly)
- "AC Tune-Up" (flat-rate service)
- "Emergency Service Call" (service fee)

**Characteristics:**
- No inventory tracking
- May be priced per-hour or flat-rate
- Often non-taxable (depending on location)

---

### Materials / Consumables

Small items used during work (often not tracked individually).

**Examples:**
- Screws and fasteners
- Wire
- Pipe fittings
- Sealant / caulk

**Characteristics:**
- Low cost (often bundled as "materials fee")
- May be tracked in inventory or added as markup percentage

---

## Item Pricing

### Fixed Price (Per Unit)

Most common pricing model.

**How it works:**
- Set a **Unit Price** (e.g., $800 for a water heater)
- Multiply by **Quantity**
- **Total = Quantity × Unit Price**

**Example:**
- Item: "Water Heater - 50 Gallon"
- Unit Price: $800
- Quantity: 1
- **Total: $800**

<!-- Screenshot: fixed-price-item.png — Item row showing unit price and quantity -->

---

### Hourly Pricing (Per Hour)

For labor or time-based services.

**How it works:**
- Set a **Unit Price** (e.g., $120/hour)
- Set **Quantity** to hours worked
- **Total = Hours × Hourly Rate**

**Example:**
- Item: "Labor - Plumbing"
- Unit Price: $120/hour
- Quantity: 2.5 hours
- **Total: $300**

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** For hourly items, enter fractional quantities (e.g., 2.5 for 2 hours 30 minutes).
</Tip>

---

### Flat-Rate Services

Single fixed price regardless of time or materials.

**How it works:**
- Set a **Unit Price** (e.g., $250 for AC tune-up)
- **Quantity: 1**
- **Total = Unit Price**

**Example:**
- Item: "AC Tune-Up (Flat Rate)"
- Unit Price: $250
- Quantity: 1
- **Total: $250**

<Info>
  Flat-rate pricing is great for predictable services. Clients love knowing the price upfront.
</Info>

---

### Cost vs Price (Margin Calculation)

FieldCamp tracks both **cost** (what you paid) and **price** (what you charge).

**Example:**
- Item: "Water Heater - 50 Gallon"
- **Cost:** $600 (what you paid the vendor)
- **Price:** $800 (what you charge the client)
- **Margin:** $200 (price - cost)
- **Markup:** 33% ((price - cost) / cost)

<!-- Screenshot: item-cost-price-margin.png — Item detail showing cost, price, and margin -->

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Use cost tracking to ensure you're profitable. If your margin is too low, adjust your pricing.
</Tip>

## Discounts

FieldCamp supports two types of discounts:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Job-Level Discount" icon="percent">
    Applied to the entire job (affects all items)
    
    **Example:** 10% off entire job
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Item-Level Discount" icon="tag">
    Applied to a single item
    
    **Example:** $50 off this specific item
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

---

### Job-Level Discount

**When to use:**
- Discount for loyal clients
- Seasonal promotions
- Bundled service discounts

**How to apply:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Go to Job Detail">
    Open the job and go to the **Details** tab.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Apply Discount">
    Below the items table, click **Apply Discount**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Choose Type">
    <Tabs>
      <Tab title="Percentage">
        Enter a percentage (e.g., 10%)
        
        **Calculation:** Subtotal × 10% = Discount amount
      </Tab>
      
      <Tab title="Fixed Amount">
        Enter a dollar amount (e.g., $100)
        
        **Calculation:** Flat $100 off
      </Tab>
    </Tabs>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Apply**. Discount appears in pricing summary.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<!-- Screenshot: apply-job-discount-modal.png — Discount modal with percentage and fixed options -->

**Pricing with job-level discount:**

| Field | Amount |
|-------|--------|
| **Subtotal** | $1,200.00 |
| **Discount (10%)** | -$120.00 |
| **Subtotal After Discount** | $1,080.00 |
| **Tax (10%)** | $108.00 |
| **Total** | $1,188.00 |

<Warning>
  **Note:** Job-level discounts apply to the subtotal **before** tax. Tax is calculated on the discounted amount.
</Warning>

---

### Item-Level Discount

**When to use:**
- Discount on a specific product (clearance item, damaged box)
- Special pricing for one service

**How to apply:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click Edit Item">
    In the items table, click **Edit** on the item row.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Scroll to Discount">
    In the edit modal, find the **Discount** field.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enter Discount">
    Choose percentage or fixed amount.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Save**. Item price updates.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<!-- Screenshot: item-discount-field.png — Edit item modal showing discount field -->

**Pricing with item-level discount:**

| Item | Unit Price | Discount | Subtotal | Tax | Total |
|------|-----------|----------|----------|-----|-------|
| Water Heater | $800.00 | -$100 (damaged box) | $700.00 | $70.00 | $770.00 |

---

### Discount Restrictions

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Job-level vs item-level discounts">
    **They don't stack.** If you apply a job-level discount, item-level discounts are ignored.
    
    **Why?** To prevent double-discounting.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Negative discounts not allowed">
    You can't enter a negative discount (to mark up an item). Use price adjustment instead.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Discount cannot exceed subtotal">
    You can't discount more than 100%. FieldCamp will warn you if you try.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Taxes

FieldCamp supports flexible tax management.

### Tax Rates

**Tax rates** are defined in [Settings → Tax Rates](/settings/tax-rates).

**Common examples:**
- Sales Tax (8.5%)
- GST (5%)
- VAT (20%)
- No Tax (0%)

---

### Taxing Items

You can set tax per-item or per-job.

#### Per-Item Tax

Each item can have a different tax rate.

**Example:**
- **Product:** Water heater (taxable at 8.5%)
- **Service:** Labor (non-taxable at 0%)

**How to set:**
1. Edit the item
2. Select **Tax Rate** from dropdown
3. Save

<!-- Screenshot: item-tax-dropdown.png — Tax rate selector for item -->

---

#### Job-Level Tax

Apply the same tax rate to all items.

**How to set:**
1. In job detail, click **Tax Settings**
2. Choose **Apply to all items**
3. Select tax rate
4. Save

<Warning>
  **Note:** If you change the job-level tax, it overrides per-item tax rates.
</Warning>

---

### Tax-Inclusive vs Tax-Exclusive

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Tax-Exclusive (Default)">
    Tax is added to the price.
    
    **Example:**
    - Item price: $100
    - Tax (10%): $10
    - **Client pays: $110**
    
    **Most common in the U.S.**
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Tax-Inclusive">
    Tax is included in the price.
    
    **Example:**
    - Item price: $110 (includes $10 tax)
    - Tax (10%): $10
    - **Client pays: $110**
    
    **Common in Europe (VAT), Australia (GST).**
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**How to set:**
1. Go to [Settings → Tax Settings](/settings/tax-settings)
2. Choose **Tax-Inclusive** or **Tax-Exclusive**
3. Save

---

### Multiple Taxes (Compound Tax)

Some regions have multiple tax rates.

**Example (Canada):**
- **GST:** 5%
- **PST:** 7%
- **Total tax:** 12%

**How to set:**
1. Go to [Settings → Tax Rates](/settings/tax-rates)
2. Click **+ Add Tax Rate**
3. Create "GST" (5%)
4. Create "PST" (7%)
5. When adding items, select both tax rates

<!-- Screenshot: multiple-tax-rates.png — Item with two tax rates applied -->

---

### Tax Exemptions

Some clients are tax-exempt (nonprofits, government agencies, resale).

**How to set:**
1. Open the **Client** profile
2. Check **Tax Exempt**
3. Upload tax exemption certificate (optional)
4. Save

**Result:** All jobs for this client are automatically non-taxable.

<Info>
  Learn more: [Client Management - Tax Exemptions](/features/clients/overview#tax-exemptions)
</Info>

## Reordering Items

Change the order items appear in the job (and on invoices).

**How to reorder:**
1. Go to job **Details** tab
2. Hover over an item row
3. Drag the **⋮⋮** handle up or down
4. Release to drop in new position

<!-- Screenshot: item-reorder-drag.png — Item being dragged to new position -->

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Put high-value items or primary services first. They appear first on invoices.
</Tip>

## Removing Items

Delete an item from a job.

**How to remove:**
1. Click **⋮** on the item row
2. Click **Remove**
3. Confirm

<Warning>
  **Note:** If the job is already invoiced, removing items doesn't update the invoice. Edit the invoice directly instead.
</Warning>

## Item-Level Permissions

Control who can edit pricing and items:

| Role | Can Add Items? | Can Change Price? | Can Remove Items? |
|------|---------------|------------------|------------------|
| **Owner** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| **Admin** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes |
| **Manager** | ✅ Yes | ✅ Yes | ⚠️ Draft jobs only |
| **Technician** | ⚠️ Assigned jobs only | ❌ No | ❌ No |

<Warning>
  **Note:** Once a job is invoiced, only Owners and Admins can change items.

## Troubleshooting

### I can't find an item in the catalog

**Solution:**
1. Check spelling
2. Try searching by category or SKU
3. If it doesn't exist, add it to your catalog first from [Products & Services](/features/product-service/overview)

---

### Tax isn't calculating correctly

**Possible causes:**
1. **Item not marked taxable** — Edit the item and select a tax rate
2. **Client is tax-exempt** — Check the client's profile for tax exemption
3. **Wrong tax rate selected** — Edit the item and choose correct rate

---

### Discount isn't applying

**Possible causes:**
1. **Job-level discount conflicts with item-level discount** — Only one type can apply at a time
2. **Discount exceeds 100%** — FieldCamp blocks discounts over 100%

---

### Inventory isn't deducting

**Possible causes:**
1. **Auto-deduct disabled** — Enable in [Settings → Inventory](/settings/inventory)
2. **Job not marked complete** — Inventory only deducts when job status = Completed
3. **Item not linked to inventory** — Check that the item has inventory tracking enabled

## API: Manage Job Items

Add items to a job via the API:

```bash
curl -X POST "https://app.fieldcamp.ai/api/v1/jobs/507f1f77bcf86cd799439012/items" \
  -H "X-Api-Key: your_api_key" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "itemId": "507f1f77bcf86cd799439020",
    "quantity": 1,
    "unitPrice": 800.00,
    "taxRate": 0.10
  }'
```

```json
// Response
{
  "success": true,
  "data": {
    "jobId": "507f1f77bcf86cd799439012",
    "itemId": "507f1f77bcf86cd799439020",

    "quantity": 1,
    "unitPrice": 800.00,
    "subtotal": 800.00,
    "tax": 80.00,
    "total": 880.00
  }
}
```
