## Overview

Job photos are visual documentation of your work—before, during, and after. Capture site conditions, equipment tags, completed work, and damage reports directly from your mobile device or desktop.

Photos help you:
- **Prove work completed** to customers
- **Document site conditions** before starting work
- **Track equipment** (serial numbers, model tags)
- **Support insurance claims** with damage photos
- **Improve estimates** with visual references
- **Train new techs** with real-world examples

## Photo Types

### Before Photos

Capture initial site conditions before work begins.

**Use cases:**
- Pre-existing damage (walls, floors, equipment)
- Old equipment condition (before replacement)
- Site layout (for reference during work)
- Safety hazards (mark with annotations)

<Tip>
**Best practice:** Always take before photos for insurance jobs, water damage, or any work involving structural changes.
</Tip>

### During Work Photos

Document the work in progress.

**Use cases:**
- Installation steps (for QA or training)
- Hidden work (behind walls, under floors)
- Parts used (for inventory tracking)
- Team photos (proof of crew on site)

### After Photos

Show completed work to prove job quality.

**Use cases:**
- Completed installations
- Repaired equipment
- Clean worksite (show you left it tidy)
- Customer approval (sign-off photos)

### Equipment Photos

Tag equipment for maintenance tracking.

**Use cases:**
- Model and serial number tags
- Nameplate photos (HVAC units, water heaters)
- Equipment condition (for service history)
- Warranty documentation

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/jobs/equipment-tag-photo.png" alt="Equipment nameplate photo with serial number" />
</Frame>

### Damage Photos

Document pre-existing or new damage.

**Use cases:**
- Insurance claims
- Liability protection (damage existed before you arrived)
- Customer disputes (photo evidence)

## Capturing Photos

### Mobile App (Primary Method)

Most techs capture photos from the FieldCamp mobile app while on site.

**Steps:**
1. Open the job in the mobile app
2. Tap **Photos** tab
3. Tap **+ Add Photo** button
4. Choose:
   - **Take Photo** (opens camera)
   - **Choose from Library** (existing photos)
5. Photo uploads immediately (or queued if offline)

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/jobs/mobile-photo-capture.png" alt="Mobile app photo capture interface" />
</Frame>

**Photo capture tips:**
- Hold phone horizontally (landscape mode)
- Ensure good lighting (use flash if needed)
- Get close-ups of equipment tags (readable serial numbers)
- Include context (wide shot + detail shot)

<Info>
**Offline mode:** Photos captured offline are queued and auto-upload when internet connection is restored. Look for the cloud upload icon in the photo thumbnail.
</Info>

### Desktop Upload

Upload photos from your computer or camera.

**Steps:**
1. Open job in FieldCamp web app
2. Go to **Files** tab
3. Click **Upload Photos**
4. Drag-and-drop or browse to select files
5. Add captions and tags
6. Click **Upload**

**Supported formats:**
- JPG/JPEG (recommended)
- PNG
- HEIC (iPhone photos, auto-converted)
- WebP

<Warning>
**File size limit:** 10 MB per photo. Large files are auto-compressed to save storage space.
</Warning>

### Folders & Categories

Organize photos by type or location.

- During
- After

- Damage
- Other

**Custom folders:**
Create custom folders for specific needs:

- "Warranty Documentation"

**How to create folders:**
1. Go to job **Files** tab
2. Click **New Folder**
3. Name the folder
4. Drag photos into the folder

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/jobs/photo-folders.png" alt="Photo organization with folders" />
</Frame>

### Photo Tags

Tag photos for easy searching and filtering.

**Common tags:**
- #before, #after, #during
- #equipment, #serial-number
- #damage, #repair
- #customer-approval
- #warranty

**How to tag:**
1. Click a photo to open detail view
2. Click **Add Tag** field
3. Type tag name (autocomplete suggests existing tags)
4. Press Enter

**Searching by tag:**
- Search bar: `tag:before` shows all before photos
- Filter panel: Select multiple tags to show only matching photos

### Photo by Visit

If a job has multiple visits, organize photos by visit.

**Automatic organization:**
- Photos uploaded during Visit 1 → automatically filed under Visit 1
- Photos uploaded during Visit 2 → filed under Visit 2

**Manual organization:**
1. Click photo to open detail view
2. Change **Visit** dropdown to correct visit
3. Save

<Tip>
**Before/after by visit:** For multi-visit jobs (installation + follow-up), tag photos with visit number: "Before - Visit 1", "After - Visit 1", "Before - Visit 2", etc.
</Tip>

## Sharing Photos

### Share with Customers

**Via Client Portal:**
Photos tagged as "Customer-visible" appear in the client portal automatically.

**How to make photos customer-visible:**
1. Click photo → **Visibility**
2. Toggle **Visible to Customer** to ON
3. Save

<Info>
**Default visibility:** Before and After photos are customer-visible by default. Equipment tags and damage photos are internal-only (unless you change visibility).
</Info>

**Via Email/SMS:**
1. Select photos (checkboxes)
2. Click **Share**
3. Choose **Email** or **SMS**
4. Enter recipient email/phone
5. Add message (optional)
6. Click **Send**

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/jobs/share-photos-email.png" alt="Share photos via email interface" />
</Frame>

### Share with Team

**Internal notes:**
Attach photos to internal notes:
1. Add a note to the job
2. Click **Attach Photos**
3. Select photos from job gallery
4. Post note

**@Mentions:**
Notify team members about specific photos:
```
@john Check the equipment tag photo - serial number doesn't match invoice
```

### Include in Invoices

Attach before/after photos to invoices:

1. Create or edit invoice
2. Go to **Attachments** section
3. Select photos from job gallery
4. Photos appear in invoice PDF (below line items)

<Tip>
**Impress customers:** Always include before/after photos in invoices. It proves the work was done and justifies the price.
</Tip>

### Include in Estimates

Attach photos to estimates:

1. Create estimate from job
2. Select **Include Photos** checkbox
3. Choose which photos to include
4. Photos appear in estimate PDF

## Photo Compression & Storage

### Automatic Compression

FieldCamp auto-compresses photos to save storage and bandwidth:

| Photo Size | Compression |
|------------|-------------|
| < 2 MB | None (kept original) |
| 2-5 MB | Compressed to ~1.5 MB |
| 5-10 MB | Compressed to ~2 MB |
| > 10 MB | Rejected (too large) |

**Quality:**
- Compression uses smart algorithms to preserve quality
- Serial numbers and text remain readable
- No visible quality loss for before/after photos

<Info>
**Original quality option:** Enterprise plans can enable "Keep Originals" to store full-resolution photos (uses 3x more storage).
</Info>

### Storage Limits

| Plan | Photo Storage | Photos per Job |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| **Starter** | 5 GB | 50 photos |
| **Pro** | 50 GB | Unlimited |
| **Enterprise** | 500 GB | Unlimited |

**What happens when limit reached:**
- New photo uploads are blocked
- Notification sent to account owner
- Options: Upgrade plan or delete old photos

## Deleting Photos

### Delete Single Photo

1. Click photo to open detail view
2. Click **Delete** (trash icon)
3. Confirm deletion
4. Photo moves to Trash (recoverable for 30 days)

### Bulk Delete

1. Select multiple photos (checkboxes)
2. Click **Delete Selected**
3. Confirm deletion
4. Photos move to Trash

### Recover Deleted Photos

1. Go to **Files → Trash**
2. Select photos to recover
3. Click **Restore**
4. Photos return to original location

**Auto-deletion:**
- Photos in Trash are permanently deleted after 30 days
- Option to manually **Empty Trash** (immediate permanent deletion)

## Exporting Photos

### Download Photos

**Single photo:**
1. Click photo → **Download**
2. Photo downloads to your device

**Multiple photos:**
1. Select photos (checkboxes)
2. Click **Download Selected**
3. Photos download as a .zip file

**All job photos:**
1. Go to job Files tab
2. Click **Download All Photos**
3. Zip file downloads (organized by folder)

### Export to Cloud Storage

Automatically sync photos to Google Drive, Dropbox, or OneDrive:

1. Go to **Settings → Integrations → Cloud Storage**
2. Connect your cloud account
3. Enable **Auto-sync Job Photos**
4. Choose sync folder structure:
   - By client
   - By job
   - By date

**Sync frequency:**
- Real-time (uploads immediately)
- Daily (batch upload at midnight)
- Weekly (batch upload on Sundays)

<Tip>
**Backup strategy:** Enable cloud sync to create automatic offsite backups of all job photos. Helps with disaster recovery and long-term archival.
</Tip>

## Best Practices

### ✅ Do This

- **Capture before photos FIRST** (before touching anything)
- **Take wide + detail shots** (context + close-ups)
- **Use good lighting** (flash or natural light)
- **Include equipment tags** (model, serial number)
- **Add captions** (brief description of what's shown)
- **Tag photos immediately** (easier than organizing later)
- **Create before/after comparisons** for completed jobs

### ❌ Avoid This

- **Don't skip before photos** (can't prove pre-existing conditions later)
- **Don't upload customer faces** without permission (privacy concerns)
- **Don't take photos of sensitive info** (credit cards, SSN, medical records)
- **Don't upload low-quality/blurry photos** (reflects poorly on professionalism)
- **Don't forget to mark visibility** (internal vs customer-visible)

## Troubleshooting

### Photos Not Uploading (Mobile)

**Possible causes:**
- No internet connection
- Photo too large (>10 MB)
- App storage full
- Camera permissions denied

**Solutions:**
1. Check internet connection (Wi-Fi or cellular data)
2. Reduce photo quality in camera settings
3. Clear app cache: Settings → Apps → FieldCamp → Clear Cache
4. Grant camera permissions: Phone Settings → Apps → FieldCamp → Permissions → Camera (Allow)

### Photos Appear Rotated

**Cause:**
Some cameras don't save rotation data correctly (EXIF orientation).

**Solution:**
1. Click photo → **Edit**
2. Click **Rotate** button
3. Save

Or: Take photos in landscape mode (horizontal) to avoid rotation issues.

### Can't See Photos in Client Portal

**Cause:**
Photo visibility is set to "Internal Only".

**Solution:**
1. Click photo → **Visibility**
2. Toggle **Visible to Customer** to ON
3. Save

## API Integration

Upload and retrieve photos programmatically.

### Upload Photo

```bash
curl -X POST https://api.fieldcamp.ai/v1/jobs/{job_id}/photos \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY" \
  -F "file=@/path/to/photo.jpg" \
  -F "caption=Before photo - water heater" \
  -F "folder=before" \
  -F "tags=before,equipment,water-heater"
```

**Response:**
```json
{
  "photo_id": "photo_123",
  "url": "https://cdn.fieldcamp.ai/photos/photo_123.jpg",
  "thumbnail_url": "https://cdn.fieldcamp.ai/photos/photo_123_thumb.jpg",
  "caption": "Before photo - water heater",
  "folder": "before",
  "tags": ["before", "equipment", "water-heater"],
  "uploaded_at": "2026-02-26T15:45:00Z",
  "uploaded_by": "tech_456"
}
```

### List Job Photos

```bash
curl -X GET https://api.fieldcamp.ai/v1/jobs/{job_id}/photos \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY"
```

### Delete Photo

```bash
curl -X DELETE https://api.fieldcamp.ai/v1/jobs/{job_id}/photos/{photo_id} \
  -H "Authorization: Bearer YOUR_API_KEY"
```

<Tip>
See full API documentation at [Photos API Reference](/api-reference/photos).

## FAQ

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Can I upload videos?">
    Not currently. FieldCamp supports photos (JPG, PNG, HEIC) only. Video support is on the roadmap for 2026.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are photos backed up?">
    Yes. All photos are stored with redundancy (3 copies across multiple servers). Additionally, you can enable auto-sync to Google Drive/Dropbox for offsite backups.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can customers delete photos?">
    No. Customers can view photos (if marked customer-visible) but cannot delete or edit them. Only team members with appropriate permissions can delete photos.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Do photos count toward my storage limit?">
    Yes. Photos count toward your plan's storage limit. Starter plan includes 5 GB, Pro includes 50 GB, Enterprise includes 500 GB.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Can I take photos offline?">
    Yes. Mobile app allows photo capture without internet. Photos are queued and auto-upload when connection is restored.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Are photos compressed? Will I lose quality?">
    Photos over 2 MB are automatically compressed to save storage. Compression is smart—serial numbers and text remain readable. Enterprise plans can disable compression to keep originals.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>
