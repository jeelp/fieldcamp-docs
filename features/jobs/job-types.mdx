# Job Types

FieldCamp supports different job types for different business models. Whether you're doing one-off repairs, recurring maintenance, or long-term service contracts, FieldCamp has you covered.

<Info>
  **New to jobs?** Read [Jobs Overview](/features/jobs/overview) first to understand the basics.
</Info>

---

## Job Type Overview

There are **two primary job types** in FieldCamp:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="One-Time Jobs" icon="circle-1">
    Single projects with a clear start and end
    
    **Examples:**
    - Fix a leaky faucet
    - Install an AC unit
    - Repair a broken outlet
    
    **Default type** for most jobs
  </Card>
  
  <Card title="Recurring Jobs" icon="repeat">
    Ongoing work that repeats on a schedule
    
    **Examples:**
    - Monthly pool service
    - Quarterly HVAC maintenance
    - Weekly lawn care
    
    Automatically creates visits based on recurrence pattern
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## One-Time Jobs

### What are One-Time Jobs?

One-time jobs are single projects — work that happens once and then the job is closed.

**Characteristics:**
- Single service call or project
- Has a defined end (work is completed, invoice is paid)
- Does not repeat automatically

**Examples:**
- Emergency repair (burst pipe)
- Installation (new water heater)
- Inspection (home buyer inspection)
- Renovation project (bathroom remodel)

---

### When to Use One-Time Jobs

Use one-time jobs for:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Emergency / Reactive Work">
    Client calls with a problem, you fix it, job is done.
    
    **Examples:**
    - AC breakdown
    - Plumbing leak
    - Electrical outlet not working
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Project-Based Work">
    Work with a defined scope and end date.
    
    **Examples:**
    - Kitchen renovation
    - New HVAC system installation
    - Deck construction
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Inspections">
    One-time assessments or audits.
    
    **Examples:**
    - Home inspection
    - Equipment inspection
    - Safety audit
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

---

### Creating One-Time Jobs

**How to create:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + New Job">
    From the Jobs page or dashboard.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Job Type: One-Time">
    This is the default — no need to change anything.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in Details">
    - Client
    - Description
    - Items (optional)
    - Schedule visit (optional)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Job">
    Click **Create Job**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Detailed walkthrough: [Creating Jobs](/features/jobs/creating-jobs)
</Info>

<!-- Screenshot: create-one-time-job.png — Create job modal with one-time selected -->

---

### One-Time Jobs with Multiple Visits

**Important:** One-time jobs can have **multiple visits**.

**Example:**
- **Job:** "Bathroom Renovation"
- **Visit 1:** Demolition and framing
- **Visit 2:** Plumbing and electrical
- **Visit 3:** Drywall and tile
- **Visit 4:** Final inspection and walkthrough

**Why not create 4 separate jobs?**
- Keeps all work for one project together
- Single invoice at the end
- Easier client communication
- Better project tracking

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Use a single job for a project. Add multiple visits for different phases or days of work.
</Tip>

## Recurring Jobs

### What are Recurring Jobs?

Recurring jobs are ongoing work that repeats on a schedule — daily, weekly, monthly, quarterly, or yearly.

**Characteristics:**
- Repeat automatically based on a pattern
- Auto-create visits (optional)
- Have a start date and optional end date
- Common for maintenance contracts

**Examples:**
- Monthly pool service
- Quarterly HVAC maintenance
- Weekly lawn mowing
- Bi-weekly office cleaning
- Annual fire extinguisher inspection

<!-- Screenshot: recurring-job-example.png — Job detail showing recurring pattern -->

---

### When to Use Recurring Jobs

Use recurring jobs for:

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Maintenance Contracts">
    Ongoing service agreements with clients.
    
    **Examples:**
    - Monthly HVAC filter changes
    - Quarterly pest control
    - Annual equipment inspections
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Subscription Services">
    Services billed on a recurring basis.
    
    **Examples:**
    - Weekly lawn care
    - Bi-weekly pool service
    - Monthly facility maintenance
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Preventive Maintenance">
    Scheduled maintenance to prevent breakdowns.
    
    **Examples:**
    - Quarterly HVAC tune-ups
    - Annual backflow testing
    - Semi-annual gutter cleaning
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Regular Inspections">
    Required or recommended periodic inspections.
    
    **Examples:**
    - Annual fire safety inspections
    - Monthly elevator inspections
    - Quarterly electrical system checks
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

---

### Creating Recurring Jobs

**How to create:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Click + New Job">
    From the Jobs page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Job Type: Recurring">
    Click the **Recurring** radio button.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Configure Recurrence Pattern">
    Choose frequency: Daily, Weekly, Monthly, Quarterly, Yearly, Custom.
    
    See [Recurrence Patterns](#recurrence-patterns) below.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set End Condition">
    Choose when recurring stops:
    - Never (ongoing)
    - After X occurrences
    - Until a specific date
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Enable Auto-Create Visits (Optional)">
    Check this box to automatically schedule visits based on the pattern.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Fill in Other Details">
    Client, description, items, etc.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Create Job">
    Click **Create Job**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
  Detailed walkthrough: [Creating Jobs](/features/jobs/creating-jobs#creating-recurring-jobs)
</Info>

## Recurrence Patterns

### Daily Recurrence

**Use for:** Work that happens every day or every X days.

**Options:**
- **Every 1 day** (daily)
- **Every 2 days** (every other day)
- **Every X days** (custom interval)

**Example:**
- Daily pool service for a hotel
- Every 3 days: equipment monitoring

<!-- Screenshot: daily-recurrence-selector.png — Daily recurrence options -->

---

### Weekly Recurrence

**Use for:** Work that happens on specific days of the week.

**Options:**
- **Every X weeks**
  - 1 week (weekly)
  - 2 weeks (bi-weekly)
  - 3 weeks
  - etc.
- **On specific days**
  - Monday, Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday, Friday, Saturday, Sunday
  - Select multiple days

**Examples:**
- **Weekly lawn care every Friday**
  - Frequency: Every 1 week
  - On: Friday
- **Bi-weekly pool service on Mondays and Thursdays**
  - Frequency: Every 2 weeks
  - On: Monday, Thursday

<!-- Screenshot: weekly-recurrence-selector.png — Weekly recurrence with day-of-week picker -->

---

### Monthly Recurrence

**Use for:** Work that happens monthly.

**Options:**

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Day of Month">
    **Example:** "15th of every month"
    
    - **Day:** 1–31
    - **Every X months:** 1 (monthly), 2 (bi-monthly), 3 (quarterly), etc.
    
    **Use case:** Monthly maintenance on the 1st of every month
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Day of Week">
    **Example:** "First Monday of every month" or "Last Friday of every month"
    
    - **Week:** First, Second, Third, Fourth, Last
    - **Day:** Monday, Tuesday, etc.
    - **Every X months:** 1, 2, 3, etc.
    
    **Use case:** First Monday of every month for inspections
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

**Examples:**
- **Monthly HVAC filter change on the 1st**
  - Day of month: 1
  - Every 1 month
- **Quarterly board meeting on the first Tuesday**
  - Day of week: First Tuesday
  - Every 3 months

<!-- Screenshot: monthly-recurrence-selector.png — Monthly options with day-of-month and day-of-week -->

---

### Quarterly Recurrence

**Use for:** Work that happens every 3 months.

**Options:**
- **Starting month:** January, February, March, etc.
- **Day of month:** 1–31
- **OR Day of week:** First/Last Monday, etc.

**Examples:**
- **Quarterly HVAC tune-up on the 15th**
  - Starting: January
  - Day: 15
  - Result: Jan 15, Apr 15, Jul 15, Oct 15
- **Quarterly inspection on the first Monday**
  - Starting: January
  - Day of week: First Monday
  - Result: First Monday of Jan, Apr, Jul, Oct

<!-- Screenshot: quarterly-recurrence-selector.png — Quarterly options -->

---

### Yearly Recurrence

**Use for:** Work that happens once a year.

**Options:**
- **Month:** January–December
- **Day:** 1–31
- **OR Day of week:** First/Last Monday of the month

**Examples:**
- **Annual fire inspection on March 1**
  - Month: March
  - Day: 1
- **Annual HVAC tune-up on the first Monday of May**
  - Month: May
  - Day of week: First Monday

<!-- Screenshot: yearly-recurrence-selector.png — Yearly options -->

---

### Custom Recurrence

**Use for:** Unusual patterns that don't fit the above.

**Options:**
- **Every X days**
- **Every X weeks** (with custom day selection)
- **Every X months**

**Examples:**
- **Every 45 days**
  - Frequency: Days
  - Interval: 45
- **Every 6 weeks on Wednesdays**
  - Frequency: Weeks
  - Interval: 6
  - On: Wednesday

<!-- Screenshot: custom-recurrence-selector.png — Custom interval picker -->

## End Conditions

Every recurring job needs an end condition:

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Never">
    **Job repeats indefinitely** until manually stopped.
    
    **Use for:**
    - Ongoing maintenance contracts with no end date
    - Subscription services
    
    **Example:** Monthly pool service for a resort (ongoing)
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="After X Occurrences">
    **Job stops after a specific number of visits.**
    
    **Use for:**
    - Contracts with a set number of visits
    - Limited-time services
    
    **Example:** "12 monthly visits" for a 1-year maintenance contract
    
    **Calculation:**
    - Monthly job with 12 occurrences = 12 months = 1 year
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Until a Specific Date">
    **Job stops after a certain date.**
    
    **Use for:**
    - Contracts with a fixed end date
    - Seasonal services
    
    **Example:** "Until December 31, 2026" for a calendar-year contract
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<!-- Screenshot: end-condition-selector.png — End condition options with date picker -->

<Warning>
  **Note:** If you choose "Never", you'll need to manually cancel or pause the recurring job when it's no longer needed.
</Warning>

## Auto-Creating Visits

When you create a recurring job, you can choose to **auto-create visits**.

### How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Enable Auto-Create">
    Check **Auto-create visits** when creating the recurring job.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="FieldCamp Schedules Visits in Advance">
    Visits are created automatically based on your recurrence pattern.
    
    **Default:** Visits are created 30 days in advance (configurable in settings).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Visits Appear on Calendar">
    Auto-created visits show up on the calendar, in the mobile app, and in the job's Visits tab.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assign Techs">
    You can assign techs manually or let the AI Dispatcher recommend techs for each visit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Auto-create visits saves you from manually scheduling every occurrence. It's a huge time-saver for recurring work.
</Tip>

---

### Manual Scheduling (Without Auto-Create)

If you **don't** enable auto-create visits:

- The job is marked as recurring
- You manually schedule each visit as needed
- Useful if scheduling depends on client availability or other factors

<Info>
  Learn more: [Recurring Visits](/features/jobs/visits/recurring-visits)
</Info>

## Managing Recurring Jobs

### Viewing Recurring Jobs

Filter jobs by type to see only recurring jobs:

1. Go to **Jobs** page
2. Click **Advanced Filters**
3. Filter by **Job Type: Recurring**

<!-- Screenshot: recurring-jobs-filter.png — Filter panel with recurring job type selected -->

---

### Editing a Recurring Job

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Edit Single Occurrence">
    Change one visit without affecting the rest.
    
    **How:**
    1. Open the job
    2. Go to **Visits** tab
    3. Click the visit you want to edit
    4. Click **Edit This Visit Only**
    5. Make changes (date, time, assigned tech, etc.)
    6. Save
    
    **Result:** Only this visit is changed. Future visits remain on the original schedule.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Edit All Future Occurrences">
    Change all upcoming visits from a certain date forward.
    
    **How:**
    1. Open the job
    2. Go to **Visits** tab
    3. Click any visit
    4. Click **Edit This and Future Visits**
    5. Make changes (time, assigned tech, recurrence pattern, etc.)
    6. Save
    
    **Result:** This visit and all future visits are updated. Past visits remain unchanged.
  </Tab>
  
  <Tab title="Edit the Recurring Pattern">
    Change the recurrence settings (frequency, end date, etc.).
    
    **How:**
    1. Open the job
    2. Click **Edit Job** (sidebar or top-right)
    3. Update recurrence settings
    4. Save
    
    **Result:** Future visits are regenerated based on the new pattern.
    
    <Warning>
      **Note:** Changing the pattern may cancel existing scheduled visits and create new ones.
    </Warning>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

---

### Pausing a Recurring Job

**Temporarily stop** creating new visits without deleting the job.

**How to pause:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Job">
    Go to the job detail page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Pause Recurring">
    In the sidebar or under **⋮ More**, click **Pause Recurring**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Confirm the pause.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**What happens:**
- No new visits are auto-created
- Existing scheduled visits remain (you can cancel them manually if needed)
- Job status remains active

**How to resume:**
1. Click **Resume Recurring**
2. Confirm
3. New visits start getting created again based on the pattern

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Use pause for seasonal services (e.g., lawn care pauses in winter) or when a client temporarily suspends service.
</Tip>

---

### Cancelling a Recurring Job

**Permanently stop** the recurring job and mark it as cancelled.

**How to cancel:**

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Job">
    Go to the job detail page.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Click Cancel Recurring">
    Under **⋮ More**, click **Cancel Recurring Job**.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Select Reason">
    Choose a cancellation reason:
    - Client cancelled contract
    - Service no longer needed
    - Moved to one-time jobs
    - Other (enter reason)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Confirm">
    Click **Cancel Job**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

**What happens:**
- Job status changes to **Cancelled**
- All future visits are cancelled
- Past completed visits remain in history
- No new visits are created

<Warning>
  **Cancelled recurring jobs cannot be resumed.** If you need to restart, create a new recurring job.
</Warning>

## Maintenance Contracts

Maintenance contracts are a special use case for recurring jobs.

### What are Maintenance Contracts?

Long-term service agreements where you provide regular maintenance for a fixed price.

**Characteristics:**
- Recurring visits (monthly, quarterly, etc.)
- Fixed pricing (same price every period)
- Set duration (usually 1–3 years)
- Auto-invoicing (invoice after each visit or monthly)

**Examples:**
- **Annual HVAC contract:** 4 quarterly tune-ups for $800/year
- **Monthly pool service:** Weekly visits for $150/month
- **Quarterly fire inspection:** 4 visits/year for $600/year

---

### Creating a Maintenance Contract

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Recurring Job">
    - Job Type: Recurring
    - Frequency: Quarterly (or monthly, etc.)
    - End condition: After 4 occurrences (for 1 year)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Contract Items">
    Add the services included in the contract.
    
    **Example:**
    - "HVAC Tune-Up (Quarterly)" — $200 × 4 = $800/year
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Auto-Create Visits">
    Enable auto-create so visits are scheduled automatically.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Tag as Maintenance Contract">
    Add a tag like "Maintenance Contract" for easy filtering.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Up Auto-Invoicing (Optional)">
    Use workflow automation to create an invoice after each visit.
    
    <Info>Learn more: [Workflow Automation](/features/workflow-automation/overview)</Info>
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Create a **Job Template** for your maintenance contract so you can quickly create new contracts for clients.
</Tip>

<Info>
  Learn more: [Job Templates](/features/jobs/job-templates)
</Info>

## Emergency / On-Demand Jobs

Emergency jobs are one-time jobs with **high priority** and immediate dispatch.

### When to Use Emergency Jobs

Use for:
- Urgent client calls (burst pipe, AC breakdown, power outage)
- Same-day service
- After-hours emergencies

---

### Creating an Emergency Job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create One-Time Job">
    Standard one-time job.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Set Priority: Urgent">
    This makes the job stand out in lists and mobile app.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add "Emergency" Tag">
    For easy filtering.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Assign Tech Immediately">
    Use AI Dispatcher to find the closest available tech.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Schedule ASAP">
    Schedule the visit for today (or within hours).
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Set up a workflow automation: "When job tagged 'Emergency' → send push notification to all available techs."
</Tip>

## Installation Jobs

Installation jobs are one-time jobs for installing equipment or systems.

**Characteristics:**
- One-time (usually)
- May require multiple visits (site survey, install, final inspection)
- Often high-value
- Require specific skills and equipment

**Examples:**
- HVAC system installation
- Solar panel installation
- Pool installation

---

### Creating an Installation Job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create One-Time Job">
    Standard one-time job.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Multiple Visits">
    - Visit 1: Site survey and measurements
    - Visit 2: Installation day 1
    - Visit 3: Installation day 2
    - Visit 4: Final inspection
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Items">
    Add all products and labor.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Skills & Equipment">
    Specify required certifications and tools.
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Attach Forms">
    Attach inspection checklists or warranty forms.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Create a **Job Template** for common installations (HVAC install, water heater install, etc.).
</Tip>

## Inspection Jobs

Inspection jobs are one-time (or recurring) jobs for assessments and audits.

**Characteristics:**
- Short duration (1–2 hours)
- Forms/checklists required
- Often required by regulation or insurance
- May lead to additional work (if issues found)

**Examples:**
- Home buyer inspection
- Fire safety inspection
- Equipment inspection

---

### Creating an Inspection Job

<Steps>
  <Step title="Create Job">
    One-time or recurring (depending on inspection type).
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Attach Inspection Form">
    Attach a checklist form for techs to complete on-site.
    
    <Info>Learn more: [Job Forms & Checklists](/features/jobs/forms/overview)</Info>
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Add Inspection Items">
    - "Annual Fire Safety Inspection" — $150
    - "Inspection Report" — $0 (included)
  </Step>
  
  <Step title="Schedule">
    Schedule the visit.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  **Pro tip:** Use forms to capture photos, measurements, and pass/fail status. This creates a digital inspection report.
</Tip>

### Creating Custom Job Types

**How it works:**

<Steps>

## Best Practices

### ✅ DO

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Use recurring jobs for maintenance contracts">
    Don't create a new job every month manually. Set it up once as recurring and let FieldCamp handle the rest.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use tags to categorize job types">
    Tag jobs by service type (HVAC, Plumbing, etc.) for easy filtering and reporting.
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Create job templates for common types">
    If you do the same type of job often (AC tune-up, drain cleaning), create a template.
    
    <Info>Learn more: [Job Templates](/features/jobs/job-templates)</Info>
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Use one-time jobs with multiple visits for projects">
    Don't create separate jobs for each phase of a project. Keep it as one job with multiple visits.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

---

### ❌ DON'T

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Don't create a new recurring job every month">
    Use the recurring feature — it's what it's for!
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Don't mix job types in one job">
    If you're doing plumbing AND electrical work, consider whether they should be separate jobs (for tracking and invoicing).
  </Accordion>
  
  <Accordion title="Don't forget to set an end condition for recurring jobs">
    If you choose "Never", remember to manually cancel when the contract ends.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## API: Create Recurring Job

Create a recurring job via the API:

```bash
curl -X POST "https://app.fieldcamp.ai/api/v1/jobs" \
  -H "X-Api-Key: your_api_key" \
  -H "Content-Type: application/json" \
  -d '{
    "clientId": "507f1f77bcf86cd799439011",
    "description": "Monthly pool service",
    "jobType": "recurring",
    "recurrence": {
      "frequency": "monthly",
      "dayOfMonth": 1,
      "endCondition": "after",
      "occurrences": 12
    },
    "autoCreateVisits": true
  }'
```
